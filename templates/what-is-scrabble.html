What is Scrabble?




Simple

Word game where 2-4 players score points by placing tiles on the board.

Crossword

Words have to be formed by tiles in a crossword fashion, either from left to right in rows or downwards in columns.

Reputable

This game is sold in 121 countries and is available in over 30 langauges, this includes Braille and magnetic versions.

Ageless

Since the invention from 1938, till this day, Scrabble still remains well-liked by players of all ages.
Each year, the North American SCRABBLE Players Association (NASPA) hosts a National SCRABBLE Championship in a major U.S. city.
The tournament attracts more than 500 highly-skilled and competitive adult SCRABBLE players who compete in 31 rounds of one-on-one play over a five day period.

Board

The board consists of 15 x 15 grid of squares where tiles are placed to form words.

Butts, Brunot, Britain

This board game was initially invented by Alfred Mosher Butts in 1938, redesigned and marketed by James Brunot in 1948. The game was first sold in Great Britain in 1954.

Lexicon

Acceptable words to be used in the Scrabble game are referenced from a standard dictionary or lexicon. Two popular competition word lists include NASPA Word List and Collins Scrabble Words.

Educational

Scrabble helps to broaden one's vocabulary, builds intellectual capacity, enhances memory functionality and concentration skills.
